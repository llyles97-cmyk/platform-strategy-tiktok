## The Thesis

> On TikTok, quality doesn’t scale—attention does.  
> The songs that win aren’t the best songs. They’re the most *usable* ones.

Most virality analysis stops at engagement metrics — likes, shares, views.  
This project goes further, separating:

- **Attention capture** (what stops the scroll)  
- **Attention retention** (what holds it)  
- **Audio spread** (what travels beyond the original content)

On TikTok, a song can go viral without the artist ever posting a single video.  
That distinction is the entire framework.

---

## POV

Most music strategy is still built for a world where distribution is controlled.  
TikTok broke that model.

Attention is now decentralized. Audiences don’t just consume music—they *deploy* it.

The question is no longer:  
**“Do people like this song?”**

It’s:  
**“Can people use this song?”**

---

## The Attention System Framework

A five-layer model for diagnosing how music moves on TikTok — built specifically for audio, not adapted from general content strategy.

| Layer | What It Measures | Music-Specific Signal |
|------|-----------------|----------------------|
| **01 — Hook** | First 1–3 seconds | Which part of the song anchors the moment? |
| **02 — Retention** | Watch behavior | Does it delay payoff? Build tension? |
| **03 — Engagement** | Signal strength | Are people reacting to the song or the video? |
| **04 — Distribution** | Algorithm push | Organic spread vs creator amplification |
| **05 — Audio Portability** | Independent sound travel | Sound reuse, meme adoption, identity signaling |

> **Audio Portability is the differentiator.**  
> A song’s success is not just about being heard—it’s about being *used*.

## Case Studies

Three songs selected to isolate different mechanisms of the system (2024):

### 01 — Sabrina Carpenter, *Espresso*  
**Mechanism: Lyric-as-Meme Machine**

- Peak: #3 Billboard Hot 100  
- 65 weeks charting  
- 20+ countries charted #1  

**Key Insight:**  
> The most portable audio isn’t the most musical moment — it’s the most *quotable* one.

---

### 02 — Chappell Roan, *Good Luck, Babe!*  
**Mechanism: Platform-Resistant Hit**

- Peak: #4 Billboard Hot 100  
- 1B+ Spotify streams  
- 6× Platinum  

**Key Insight:**  
> Platform gatekeeping only kills songs that depend on the algorithm.  
> Community gravity creates its own distribution infrastructure.

---

### 03 — Charli XCX, *brat* (album cycle)  
**Mechanism: Aesthetic-Driven Spread**

- Merriam-Webster Word of the Year 2024  
- 80M+ SNL parody views  

**Key Insight:**  
> When audio is embedded in an identity system, it spreads as *behavior*, not just sound.

|------|-----------------|---------------|----------------------|
| Espresso | Lyric glitch | Hook | Phrase / language |
| Good Luck Babe | Community | Distribution | Narrative + performance |
| brat | Identity system | Audio Portability | Aesthetic + identity |

---

## Pattern Synthesis

Across all three cases:

1. **Confusion outperforms clarity**  
   Cognitive friction increases retention and comment volume  

2. **Portability > quality**  
   The most successful songs are the most usable, not the most polished  

3. **Multiple ignition points > one viral spike**  
   Sustained distribution beats algorithm bursts  

4. **Comments = diagnostic layer**  
   They reveal which part of the system is actually working  

---

## What I Would Do If I Ran a Label in 2026

### 1. Design for Entry Points, Not Full Songs  
Produce multiple hookable moments and test them before release  

### 2. Build Portability First, Streaming Second  
If it can’t be used without the artist, it won’t scale  

### 3. Treat TikTok as Infrastructure, Not Marketing  
Optimize for reuse and spread—not just reach  

### 4. Manufacture Multiple Ignition Points  
Plan for culture, creator, and community moments separately  

### 5. Use Comments as Real-Time Diagnostics  
They reveal what to amplify before spending on distribution
