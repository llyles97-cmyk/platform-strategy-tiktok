# Methodology

## Research Approach

This project was built framework-first, not data-first. The Attention System Framework was developed before case studies were selected — ensuring that the cases were chosen to *test* the framework rather than to confirm a predetermined narrative.

---

## Framework Development

### Why Five Layers?

Most TikTok content frameworks stop at four variables: hook, retention, engagement, distribution. These map cleanly to how TikTok's algorithm works for general content.

Music required an additional layer because of a fundamental difference: **audio is both the content and the distribution vehicle.**

A song can go viral on TikTok without the original artist ever posting. A sound can accumulate millions of uses across thousands of creators with no connection to the source. That behavior — Audio Portability — has no equivalent in non-music TikTok content and cannot be captured by the standard four-layer model.

Layer 5 is not an extension of the framework. It's the reason the framework exists for music specifically.

### Why These Five, In This Order?

The layers are sequenced to mirror the attention journey:

1. **Hook** — Does it stop the scroll?
2. **Retention** — Does it hold through the end?
3. **Engagement** — Does it generate a response?
4. **Distribution** — Does the algorithm amplify it?
5. **Audio Portability** — Does the sound travel beyond the original context?

Each layer is a prerequisite for the next. A song with no hook doesn't build retention. A song with no retention doesn't generate meaningful engagement. But Audio Portability can occasionally shortcut this sequence — a song can achieve portability before it achieves mainstream engagement (see: brat's aesthetic spreading before the album even dropped).

---

## Case Study Selection Criteria

Cases were selected to satisfy three conditions:

1. **Each case isolates a different primary mechanism** — the three cases should not be interchangeable. Each should tell a story that only it can tell.

2. **Each case has documented, publicly verifiable metrics** — no speculation about view counts or engagement rates. Every data point cited should be traceable to a published source.

3. **The three cases together should cover the full framework** — not every layer needs to be equally active in every case, but across all three cases, all five layers should be meaningfully illustrated.

### Why 2024 Specifically?

2024 was chosen because:
- The Luminate/TikTok Music Impact Report provides credible industry-level data for the year
- All three selected cases reached their peak cultural impact within the same calendar year
- The UMG/TikTok licensing dispute created a natural experiment (Good Luck, Babe!) that would not have existed in other years
- Recency signals that this analysis reflects current platform behavior, not historical patterns

---

## What This Framework Does Not Claim

- It does not predict which songs will go viral
- It does not provide a formula for engineering virality
- It does not account for paid promotion, playlist placement, or radio airplay as primary variables
- It does not claim that the five layers are the only variables that matter

What it does claim: these five layers, applied consistently, can *diagnose* why a song spread the way it did — and that diagnosis has strategic value for labels, artists, and platform teams.

---

## Limitations

- Case studies are selected from high-visibility 2024 releases; findings may not generalize to emerging artists with smaller fanbases
- Audio Portability metrics (sound uses, duet/stitch behavior) are not always publicly available at a granular level; some analysis relies on qualitative observation rather than precise counts
- TikTok's algorithm is not publicly documented; distribution layer analysis is based on observed patterns rather than confirmed platform behavior

---

## Intended Audience

This project was built for roles at the intersection of music, data, and platform strategy — specifically targeting:

- Music streaming platforms (Spotify, Apple Music, Amazon Music)
- Major and independent labels (UMG, Sony, Atlantic, Secretly Group)
- Music analytics companies (Luminate, Chartmetric, Soundcharts)
- Media and entertainment strategy teams

The framework is designed to be reusable — applied to any artist, any release, any platform cycle — not just the three cases studied here.
